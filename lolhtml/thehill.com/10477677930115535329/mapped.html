Zaid Jilani on how Biden beat Sanders on Super Tuesday 

Zaid Jilani, a journalist at the University of California Berkeley Greater Good Science Center, said on Hill TV’s Rising on Friday morning that former Vice President Joe Biden was successful on Super Tuesday in part because Sen. Bernie Sanders (I-Vt.) took key moderate states for granted. 

“What Joe Biden did right is he kept the faith, understood he had a base of support going into South Carolina and Super Tuesday, while I think Bernie started to take it for granted,” Jilani said.

 “I think Biden seized that moment to consolidate,” he added, referring to his many endorsement leading up to and after Super Tuesday. 

Sanders had the early momentum in the 2020 nomination race, winning New Hampshire and Nevada and virtually tying in Iowa.

But after a clear victory in South Carolina, Biden took 10 out of 14 states voting on Super Tuesday, resetting the primary race and surpassing Sanders in the delegate count. 

With other major candidates dropping out of the race, the Democratic nomination has now come to a two-way battle between Biden and Sanders.

Six states are set to vote next on Super Tuesday, including Michigan, the biggest electoral prize of the night.
