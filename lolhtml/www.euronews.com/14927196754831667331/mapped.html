Watch live: WHO gives latest update on coronavirus crisis

The threat of COVID-19 becoming a pandemic "has become very real" but the world is "not at the mercy of the virus," the director-general of the World Health Organization (WHO) said Monday.

Dr Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus said it was "certainly troubling" that cases of the new coronavirus had passed 100,000 in 100 countries.

However, he said it was important not to give up efforts to contain the disease, stressing that 93% of deaths from the so far are from just four countries.

“The threat of a pandemic has become very real,” he told reporters in Geneva. “But it would be the first pandemic in history that could be controlled. The bottom line is we’re not at the mercy of the virus.”

He spoke as the global economc fallout from COVID-19 worsened sharply, with markets around the world plummeting after Saudi Arabia launched an oil price war with Russia amid decreased demand.

London's FTSE100 went intro freefall after opening, losing 9%, while the Bourse in Paris lost 7%. On Wall Street, trading was suspended after the Dow fell 5.83% and the Nasdaq lost 7.12%.

The price of oil also saw its fastest single-day fall since the first Gulf War.

COVID-19 has caused major economic disruption, in particular to aviation, leading to lower demand for oil — but an OPEC meeting with Russia last week failed to see countries agree to a production cut. In response, Saudi Arabia has warned it will increase its production and slash its own prices to claw back market share.

The disease has so far infected 109,000 people and killed 3,800.
